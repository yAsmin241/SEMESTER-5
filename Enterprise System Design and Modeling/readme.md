# SECP3744 – Enterprise Systems Design and Modelling
---

### Assignment 1 – Technical Report: Risks from AI, IoT Integration, and Data Governance in Global Enterprises

Working on this technical report deepened my understanding of how AI and IoT are not just tools for efficiency, but also sources of serious risk when left ungoverned. Researching real-world examples in manufacturing and logistics helped me connect abstract concepts like data governance frameworks and GDPR compliance to actual business consequences, and co-writing the paper with a partner pushed me to think more critically about how to structure and justify arguments in an academic report format.

---

### ESDM Industry Talk PPG Reflection
Attending the industrial talk featuring Mr. Patrick Thong from PPG provided a profound bridge between theoretical enterprise concepts and real-world application, showcasing how the company coordinates its global operations across major operational streams like Demand to Replenish and Order to Cash. It was deeply inspiring to learn about their shifting infrastructure from SAP S/4HANA to SAP S/4HANA Cloud and to get a firsthand look at the high-demand career opportunities and collaborative mindset driving the PPG Digital IT Team. This session truly opened my eyes to the practical value of SAP and ERP systems within a major global coatings enterprise.

---

### SAP BTP Chatbot Project Reflection
This project provided hands-on experience in building, deploying, and monitoring enterprise-grade conversational agents using SAP Conversational AI. By configuring logic-heavy customer shipping bots with custom entities, webhooks, and intent forks, alongside deploying automated FAQ bots via structured CSV files, I learned how to turn complex back-end data into seamless user interactions. Successfully embedding the final webchat script into a Microsoft IIS web server highlighted the end-to-end technical lifecycle required to take an AI subsystem from a development environment to a live webpage.

---

### UTHM Case Study – Hostel Management System (Staff Scheduling Subsystem)

This project was the most hands-on experience I have had with enterprise architecture, as it required me to go beyond designing diagrams and actually implement a working subsystem using SAP Build Apps. Translating the TOGAF As-Is and To-Be architectures into a functioning staff scheduling system — complete with CRUD logic, entity relationships, and a responsive UI — showed me how much the design phase truly shapes the quality of implementation, and working within a low-code environment taught me to think creatively within constraints rather than around them.

---

### Overall Reflection

Across both the technical report and the case study project, the central lesson of this course has been that enterprise systems are fundamentally about alignment — aligning technology with business goals, data with governance policies, and design with real user needs. Seeing how UTHM is navigating its own digital transformation from a fragmented As-Is state toward a unified To-Be vision made these concepts feel grounded and relevant, and it reinforced my belief that thoughtful architecture, not just functional code, is what makes systems sustainable in the long run.
